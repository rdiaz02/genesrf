OOB predictions (help)



Most samples are faily well classified, specially those from class ALL. For these, their average out of bag probability of membership in their true class is larger than 0.75. There is, however, one sample from class AML (sample 32) that has an average out of bag probability of membership on its real class around 0.5. Failing to place this sample in the correct class is the reason why we see the red line in the first figure sometimes in 0.026 (0.26 = 1/38). There are another two samples in AML (28 and 35) with a probability of being missclassified around 0.4. In fact, it turns out that in some of the bootstrap samples there are two samples incorrectly classified (the red line in the first figure at values of 0.0526 = 2/38).

Importance spectrum plots (help)



The interpretation of the importance spectrum plots is still rather heuristic (more theoretical work is underway). Anyway, it seems clear that first 30 variables are certainly "important". They are above the importances from the data sets with randomly permuted class labels. Note that the first 30 variables are above not only of the importances for the variables in the same importance rank, but also above the importance of the single most important variable from the data sets with permuted class labels.

Selection probability plot (help)



If we look at the stability of the selection of the first 20 genes (blue dots) we see that, if we want to look for genes that are repeatedly choosen among the best 20, only the two or three most important ones are good candidates.
